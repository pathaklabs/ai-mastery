## What you are doing

You are recording a 60–90 second screen recording of the live dashboard. You will talk over it (or add captions) as you scroll through the project health cards, cost charts, and content tracker.

This is the most visual piece of content in the entire program. It is the one that gets shared without any reading required.

---

## Why this step matters

Some people read blog posts. More people watch a 60-second video.

The Instagram reel reaches a different audience than LinkedIn posts or blog articles. It also works differently: people share it, save it, and come back to it. A dashboard that is clearly live and clearly built by one person is genuinely surprising to most viewers.

Raw and real builds more trust than polished and staged. You do not need motion graphics or a fancy intro. You need a real system doing real things.

## Before you record

### Make the dashboard look its best

Before recording, do a quick check:

1. Open the live dashboard URL in a full-screen browser
2. Make sure all 5 project cards show "Live" (green badge)
3. Check the cost charts — if they look empty, send some test events first
4. Verify the content tracker shows your published content
5. Zoom the browser to 90% so more content fits on screen without scrolling

### Prepare a short script

Do not improvise. Read this out in 5 seconds of practice. Then record naturally.

## Step-by-step recording instructions

### Step 1 — Record the screen

**macOS built-in:**
- Press `Command + Shift + 5`
- Choose "Record Selected Portion" or "Record Entire Screen"
- Click Record
- Press `Command + Shift + 5` again when done, then click Stop

**Windows built-in:**
- Press `Windows + G` to open Xbox Game Bar
- Click the Record button (or press `Windows + Alt + R`)

**OBS (any platform — more control):**
- Download from obsproject.com
- Create a Scene with "Display Capture" as a source
- Click Start Recording
- Click Stop Recording when done

### Step 2 — Record your voiceover

**Option A — Record voice and screen together**

Use your laptop microphone while screen recording. Move away from loud environments. A quiet room with some soft background noise is fine — perfect audio is not required.

**Option B — Record screen first, voice separately**

Record the screen silently, then record just your voice reading the script, then combine them in your editing tool. This gives you more control.

### Step 3 — Edit

You do not need professional editing. The minimum is:
- Trim the start and end (remove the few seconds before and after you click record)
- Cut any awkward pauses longer than 3 seconds
- Add captions (Instagram auto-generates them — turn on "Auto-captions" when uploading)

**Free tools:**
- macOS: iMovie (free, already installed)
- Any platform: CapCut (free, good for mobile editing)
- Online: Descript (transcribes and lets you edit by text)

### Step 4 — Format for Instagram

Instagram Reels specs:
- Aspect ratio: 9:16 (vertical) — crop your 16:9 screen recording
- Duration: 60–90 seconds (can go up to 90, sweet spot is 60–75)
- Resolution: 1080×1920 preferred

To crop your 16:9 recording to 9:16:
- In iMovie or CapCut, add your screen recording to the timeline
- Set the canvas to 9:16
- Zoom in on the centre of the dashboard — the most important cards will still be visible

Alternative: record in a 9:16 window by making your browser narrower before recording. The dashboard is responsive and will reflow.

```

```

## Tips for making the reel perform

**Do not hide the imperfection.** If the pipeline card shows "Idle" because it only runs in the morning, leave it. That is honest. Viewers trust real more than perfect.

**State the cost number out loud.** It is always more interesting than people expect. "14 weeks of running 5 AI projects cost me less than two months of Netflix" is a shareable line.

**End on the dashboard URL.** Show the URL in the final frame. Some viewers will pause and type it.

**Add text overlays** for the project names as you scroll over each card. Viewers often watch reels silently — captions and text overlays reach them.

**Post on a Tuesday or Wednesday** between 9am and 11am in your target timezone. Avoid Mondays (competitive) and Fridays (low engagement).
