## The story

> As a developer building in public, I want to ship AIGA as open source and write about the experience — so that other developers, engineering managers, and compliance leads can find it, use it, and follow my work.

---

## Why this story is the highest-impact content in the whole program

AIGA sits at the intersection of two things people urgently care about in 2024–2025:

1. **The EU AI Act** — every tech company selling into Europe is scrambling to understand it
2. **Practical AI engineering** — people want to see RAG built for a real use case, not a toy demo

That combination means this blog post will reach people who are not just curious developers — it will reach CTOs, compliance leads, and engineering managers who are under real regulatory pressure. That is the audience most likely to share your work, hire you, or become customers someday.

---

## Content pieces in this story

### P4-C1 — Blog post (highest-impact post in the program)

**Title:** "How I Built an Open-Source AI Governance Assistant"

Target audience: engineering managers, CTOs, compliance leads in European tech.

Why they will read it: They are legally required to understand the EU AI Act and do not have time to read 144 pages.

What the post must include:
- EU AI Act explainer (plain English, 3–4 paragraphs)
- Architecture diagram of AIGA
- Real example of risk classification with actual JSON output
- GitHub link with quickstart

→ See [P4-C1: Blog post task](p4-c1-blog-governance.md) for full outline and instructions.

---

### P4-C2 — LinkedIn post

**Hook:** "The EU AI Act is 144 pages. I built an AI to navigate it."

This is a short-form post that shows one real output from AIGA and links to the blog.

→ See [P4-C2: LinkedIn task](p4-c2-linkedin-eu-ai-act.md) for draft template.

### P4-C3 — Instagram carousel

**Title:** "AI Governance: 5 things every tech manager needs to know"

A visual carousel that turns the most important points from the EU AI Act into plain-English slides. Each slide = one thing. End slide = link to AIGA.

Slide ideas:
- Slide 1: Title card — "5 things every tech manager needs to know about the EU AI Act"
- Slide 2: What is the EU AI Act and who it applies to
- Slide 3: The 4 risk levels (visual)
- Slide 4: What "High Risk" means for your team
- Slide 5: The documentation you need before you ship
- Slide 6: Call to action — "I built a free tool to help. Link in bio."

---

### P4-C4 — Publish GitHub repo

A good README is the difference between 3 stars and 300 stars.

The README must:
- Explain what AIGA does in 2 sentences (not 10)
- Show a screenshot of the chat interface
- Include a 5-minute quickstart (copy-paste commands that actually work)
- Link to the blog post

→ See [P4-C4: Publish repo task](p4-c4-publish-repo.md) for README template.

## Publish order

Do these in this order. Each one feeds the next.

```
Step 1: Get the repo in good shape
  └── Good README, working quickstart, screenshots taken
         │
         ▼
Step 2: Publish the repo (make public)
  └── You now have a URL to link to
         │
         ▼
Step 3: Write and publish the blog post
  └── Blog links to the repo
         │
         ▼
Step 4: Publish LinkedIn post
  └── LinkedIn post links to the blog
         │
         ▼
Step 5: Publish Instagram carousel
  └── Bio link → blog or repo
```
